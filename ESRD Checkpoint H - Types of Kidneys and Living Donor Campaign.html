KIDNEY TRANSPLANT ROAD MAP
CHECKPOINT H: Types of Kidneys
and Living Donor Campaign
NN
Deceased Donor Kidneys: A kidney from a person who has passed away. These kidneys
need to be transplanted soon after the donor has died. A transplant of this type can
happen at any time.
NN
Living Donor Kidneys: A kidney given to a family member or friend. The donor can live
a healthy life with one kidney. Sometimes, people donate a kidney out of the kindness of
their heart without knowing the recipient. A Living Donor kidney is the best choice for all
patients and will help you get a transplant sooner.
· Paired Exchange: This is when a donor has someone willing to give a kidney but
is not a good match for the recipient. They can swap with another donor and
recipient to get a good match.
NN
High KDPI Kidneys: This is a “better than dialysis kidney” that is a great option for older
patients or patients with multiple health problems.
NN
Extended Criteria Kidneys: A kidney from a donor who may have additional health risks,
such as Hepatitis C. Even this type of kidney is better than dialysis. If you are interested in
this option, talk to your transplant team about the risks and treatments available to keep
you healthy.
NN
Living Donor Campaign: You can create a living donor campaign if you don’t have a
living donor. Creating a campaign is easier when you partner with someone who knows
how to do it, like the National Kidney Foundation’s Patient Navigator program.
